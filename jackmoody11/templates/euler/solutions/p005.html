{% extends 'euler/solution.html' %} {% block problem_title %}Problem 5 - Smallest Multiple{% endblock %} {% block problem_text %} 2520 is the smallest number that can be divided by each of the numbers from 1 to 10 without any remainder. What is the smallest positive number that is evenly divisible by all of the numbers from 1 to 20? {% endblock %} {% block idea_text %} This is equivalent to the problem of finding the least common multiple of numbers from 1 to 20. Stated differently, we are finding the least amount of times we need to include each prime in the prime factorization. For example, \(2^4 = 16\) is the most number of times we have a factor of 2. While 3 has a max power of 2 under 20, and all primes greater than 3 have a maximum power of 1. Therefore, we multiple \(2^4\) by \(3^2\) and all of the other primes greater than 3 but less than 20 to find the final answer. {% endblock %}
